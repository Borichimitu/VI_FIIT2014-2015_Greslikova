1972507107<title:Autism><shortabstract:Autism is a disorder of neural development characterized by impaired social interaction, by impaired verbal and non-verbal communication, and by restricted, repetitive or stereotyped behavior. The diagnostic criteria require that symptoms become apparent before a child is three years old. Autism affects information processing in the brain by altering how nerve cells and their synapses connect and organize; how this occurs is not well understood.><categories:Autism.Communication_disorders.Mental_and_behavioural_disorders.Neurological_disorders.Neurological_disorders_in_children.Pervasive_developmental_disorders.Psychiatric_diagnosis.><redirects:<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Autistic_Disorder>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Autistic_disorder>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Early_infantile_autism>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Early_Infantile_Autism>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Infantile_autism>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Early_childhood_autism>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Early_Childhood_Autism>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Kannerian_autism>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Kanner's_syndrome>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Kanner's_Syndrome>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Kanner_syndrome>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Kanner_Syndrome>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Childhood_autism>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Childhood_Autism>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Austistic>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Authistic>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Authism>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Autisim>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Austistic>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Austism>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Auties>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Classic_autism>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Classical_autism>.<http://dbpedia.org/resource/Autism_in_india>.>
